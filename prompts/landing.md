# `/` Landing Page Prompt

```text
Responsive landing page for BoilerSub, a Purdue-only student sublease marketplace for web and mobile.

Goal:
Create a high-trust, high-energy homepage that feels like an editorial tech product rather than a generic housing site. The design should feel premium, kinetic, and modern, built for Purdue students looking for subleases near campus.

Sections:
- Fixed glassmorphic top navigation with BoilerSub wordmark, links for Explore, Listings, Sublease, and Guide, plus Sign In and primary CTA button
- Full-screen hero section with cinematic Purdue-campus background image
- Large editorial headline with strong typography and asymmetrical composition
- Supporting subheadline explaining BoilerSub as a curated Purdue housing platform
- Floating glass-style search bar with 3 fields: Location, Duration, Budget, plus primary “Explore Now” CTA
- Secondary hero actions for “3D View” and “Post Your Sublease”
- Bento-style featured listings section with one large hero listing card, one medium listing card, one stats/info card, and one technology/value card
- Large showcase section promoting 3D walkthroughs / immersive previews with strong visual storytelling
- Footer with BoilerSub branding and simple legal/product links

Visual Style:
- Editorial, modern, kinetic, asymmetrical, premium, high-trust
- Inspired by a digital magazine mixed with a modern proptech startup
- Use glassmorphism for floating UI like nav and search
- Avoid generic card-grid SaaS visuals
- No thin gray divider lines; separate sections with tonal layering and spacing instead
- Use subtle gradients and soft ambient shadows

Branding and Colors:
- Background / surface: warm off-white bone tone
- Primary: muted Purdue-inspired gold
- Secondary: electric blue for primary actions
- Tertiary: kinetic coral for urgency/highlights
- Use blue gradients on primary CTAs
- Use tonal surface layers instead of borders

Typography:
- Headlines and display text: Plus Jakarta Sans
- Body and labels: Manrope
- Hero headline should be oversized, bold, tightly tracked, and highly visual
- Use small uppercase label text for section intros

Responsive Behavior:
- Must work beautifully on both mobile and desktop
- Desktop: asymmetrical hero, wide editorial spacing, bento grid for featured content
- Mobile: stacked layout, preserved hierarchy, glass search module becomes vertical, navigation simplifies cleanly
- Maintain premium feel on small screens, not just compressed desktop

Content Direction:
- Purdue-specific positioning
- Messaging should emphasize curated subleases, trust, student relevance, and speed
- Make the product feel exclusive and campus-native

Accessibility:
- Ensure readable contrast for all text
- Touch targets should be mobile-friendly
- Keep form fields and CTA buttons clear and usable across screen sizes
```
